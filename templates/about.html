Who are We?

We are an interdisciplinary team from NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System , and ESIP's Discovery Cluster,
looking to improve search relevancy for earth science data through the concept of usage-based discovery.



What is Usage-Based Discovery?

Traditional methods of dataset discovery leverage dataset features (like platform or proccessing level) to perform search. This can be a difficult paradigm for non-experts and interdisciplinary scientists alike. Usage-Based Discovery takes a different approach by first allowing users to narrow their search by how the datasets are used in real-world applications and research (like an application that monitors forest fires or a publication that looks into the relationship between sea level rice and ice melt). By promoting these examples of data use (and their associated datasets), users can more easily get started with using the data that's suited for their goals, building off of the existing work of the scientific community. At its core, Usage-Based Discovery is a socially-driven
process, where discovering data is an open and collaborative effort spread amongst a diverse community.
